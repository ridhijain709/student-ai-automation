# Ankit Kumar — Demo Script

## Demo Name: White-Label AI Automation Partnership

## Duration: 20 minutes

## Goal: Show an agency owner how the white-label model works and why it makes business sense for them to add automation to their service offering.

---

## Setup Before the Demo

- One completed automation demo ready (WhatsApp lead flow or similar)
- A simple one-page overview of the partnership model
- Optional: a sample agency-branded demo output (shows how the system would be presented to their end client)

---

## Step 1 — Open with the Agency's World (3 minutes)

Start with:

> "When your clients finish a branding or marketing project with you — what typically comes next? Do they start asking about more digital tools, or does the relationship tend to end there?"

Let them describe their client lifecycle. Then:

> "The agencies that retain clients long-term are usually the ones that keep adding value after the initial project. Automation is one of the fastest ways to do that right now — because almost every small business has a manual communication problem that costs them real money."

---

## Step 2 — Explain the Model (5 minutes)

Walk through the partnership structure:

> "Here's how I work with agencies. When one of your clients needs automation — WhatsApp follow-up, lead handling, content scheduling — you brief me on the scope. I build it in about a week. It's delivered under your agency's brand. You charge your client the market rate, pay me the build cost, and keep the margin."

Draw or show a simple flow:

```
Agency client → Automation request
Agency → Briefs consultant
Consultant → Builds in 5–7 days
Delivered → Under agency branding
Agency → Keeps margin (₹10,000–₹18,000 per project)
```

Say:

> "You don't need to hire a developer, learn new tools, or change how you operate. You just add automation to what you already offer."

---

## Step 3 — Live Demo: Show What the System Looks Like (7 minutes)

Pick a relevant example from their client base (e.g., if they work with restaurants or D2C brands, run that demo).

Walk through:
1. Customer sends a WhatsApp message
2. AI replies instantly
3. Lead is logged to a sheet
4. Follow-up triggers after 24 hours

Say:

> "This is what you'd deliver to a client. The entire build takes one week. It costs them ₹20,000–₹25,000. Your build cost to me is ₹10,000. That's a ₹10,000–₹15,000 margin per project for your agency with no technical work from your side."

---

## Step 4 — Propose a Pilot (3 minutes)

Say:

> "I'm not asking for a commitment. Here's what I'd suggest: pick one client you're currently working with — or someone in your pipeline — who has a WhatsApp or lead management problem. Let me build the system, you deliver it. We see how it works, and if it goes well, we can make it a regular arrangement."

---

## Step 5 — Close (2 minutes)

> "The goal is simple — I help you expand your service offering without expanding your team. Your clients get more value, you earn more per account, and neither of us needs to change how we work."

Ask:

> "Is there a client type you work with a lot that might have this kind of problem?"

---

## Objection Handling

**"Our clients are small — they might not pay ₹20,000."**
> "The pricing scales — smaller clients can start at ₹8,000–₹12,000 for a basic setup. The margin is lower but the relationship value stays."

**"What if something goes wrong after delivery?"**
> "I provide a support period after every build. For ongoing support, there's a monthly retainer that the agency can include in the client's package — another recurring revenue stream."

**"We've never sold automation before."**
> "You don't need to know how it works — just know what problem it solves. I'll give you a one-page brief for each project type so you can position it to your clients confidently."
