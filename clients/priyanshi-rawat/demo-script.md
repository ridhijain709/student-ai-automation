# Priyanshi Rawat — Demo Script

## Demo Name: Personal Brand Content Engine

## Duration: 15 minutes

## Goal: Show that a week of personal brand content — captions, hashtags, posting schedule — can be generated in under 20 minutes with AI doing the writing.

---

## Setup Before the Demo

- Google Sheet content calendar template open
- Make.com scenario active and connected to Gemini or OpenAI
- A few example topics pre-filled to show the output quickly
- Buffer connection ready (or show scheduled output mock)

---

## Step 1 — Open with the Problem (2 minutes)

Ask:

> "How often do you post on LinkedIn or Instagram right now? And when you do, what's the process — do you sit down and write it from scratch each time?"

Let them describe their current approach. Then say:

> "Most people I talk to have plenty of ideas — the bottleneck is the writing and the scheduling. Let me show you what it looks like when the writing is done by AI in 30 seconds, and the scheduling happens automatically."

---

## Step 2 — Live Demo: Topic to Published Post (5 minutes)

Fill in one row in the Google Sheet:

| Platform | Topic | Tone | CTA |
|----------|-------|------|-----|
| LinkedIn | Why consistency matters more than quality in personal branding | Conversational + honest | Follow for weekly posts |

Trigger the automation. Show the AI output:

---

*Example output:*

> "The best personal brand advice I ever got wasn't about quality.
>
> It was: show up consistently.
>
> Because here's the truth — the post you publish on your worst day still helps someone. The perfect post you never publish helps no one.
>
> Consistency builds trust in a way that occasional brilliance never can. People follow those who show up reliably, not those who only appear when they have something perfect to say.
>
> What changed everything for me: I stopped trying to be perfect and started trying to be present.
>
> What's one thing you'd share more often if you stopped worrying about whether it was good enough?
>
> #personalbranding #linkedin #growth #consistency #professionaldev"

---

Say:

> "That took 25 seconds to generate. It's ready to post right now, or you can edit one or two lines to make it more personal. Most of them go out unchanged."

---

## Step 3 — Show a Full Week in One Sitting (3 minutes)

Show 5 rows in the sheet, each with a different topic. All 5 captions are already generated.

Say:

> "That's Monday through Friday — a full week of LinkedIn content. If you sit down on Sunday evening and fill in 5 topics, you're done for the week. The system writes all 5 captions and schedules them automatically."

---

## Step 4 — Show Scheduling (2 minutes)

Show or explain the Buffer connection:

> "Each approved caption goes to Buffer. Posts are scheduled for the best-performing times — typically Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday between 8–10 AM and 5–7 PM. They go out automatically. You don't need to open your phone or remember to post."

---

## Step 5 — Close (2 minutes)

> "What this means for your personal brand: consistent posts, every week, without spending hours on writing. Your ideas are the input — AI and automation do the rest."

Ask:

> "What topics do you feel like you'd post about more often if the writing wasn't taking so long?"

Use their answer to show one live generation with that exact topic.

---

## Pricing Conversation

> "Setup — including the content sheet, AI connection, scheduling integration, and a calibration session to match your voice — is ₹8,000–₹10,000. One-time cost. No subscriptions from my side. After that, the system runs on its own unless you want changes."

---

## Objection Handling

**"Will it sound like me?"**
> "After the first batch, we do a calibration round — you tell me what felt off, I adjust the prompts. After 2–3 rounds, most people can't tell the difference."

**"I don't have 5 topics a week."**
> "You don't need 5. Even 2–3 consistent posts a week is enough to build a strong presence. And once you're in the system, topics start coming naturally — you'll have more than you need."

**"I'm not sure what to post about."**
> "I can help with a content pillar framework — 4–5 themes that cover your expertise, experiences, and perspective. Once those are defined, topics flow easily."
