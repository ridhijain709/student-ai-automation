# Sitanshu Tripathi — Demo Script

## Demo Name: AI Content Scheduling System

## Duration: 15 minutes

## Goal: Show that a week's worth of professional social media content can be generated and scheduled in under 30 minutes — with AI doing the writing and automation handling the rest.

---

## Setup Before the Demo

- Google Sheet template with content calendar structure ready
- Make.com scenario connected and active
- A few example topics pre-filled to show the output format
- Buffer or scheduling tool connected (or a mock output showing scheduled posts)

---

## Step 1 — Open with the Problem (2 minutes)

Ask:

> "How often do you post on LinkedIn right now? And when you do, roughly how long does it take to decide on a topic, write the caption, add hashtags, and schedule it?"

Let them answer. Then say:

> "Most professionals I talk to say the same thing — they know they should post more, but writing content takes 20–30 minutes per post, and by the time the day is over, it doesn't happen. Let me show you what it looks like when that process takes about 2 minutes per post."

---

## Step 2 — Live Demo: Topic to Caption in 2 Minutes (5 minutes)

Open the Google Sheet and fill in one row:

| Date | Platform | Topic | Tone | CTA |
|------|----------|-------|------|-----|
| 28 Apr | LinkedIn | 3 things consulting clients never tell you upfront | Professional + direct | Follow for weekly insights |

Trigger the automation (or show the result already generated):

*Example AI output:*

> "Most consulting projects hit friction not because of the work — but because of what clients didn't say at the start.
>
> Here are 3 things I've learned to ask before signing any engagement:
>
> 1. Who actually makes the final decision here?
> 2. What's already been tried and failed?
> 3. What does success look like to you personally — not just the business?
>
> Ask these early. It changes everything.
>
> Follow for weekly consulting insights from the field.
>
> #consulting #businessstrategy #clientwork #leadership"

Say:

> "That caption was generated in under 30 seconds. It's ready to post as-is, or you can edit it — but most of them go out unchanged."

---

## Step 3 — Show the Batch Process (3 minutes)

Show 5 rows already filled in the sheet, with captions generated for all 5.

Say:

> "That's a full week of LinkedIn content planned and written in one sitting. The whole process — deciding topics, generating captions, reviewing — takes about 20 minutes."

---

## Step 4 — Show Scheduling (2 minutes)

Explain (or show) how the posts are sent to Buffer:

> "Once captions are in the sheet and approved, they go to Buffer automatically — scheduled for the optimal posting times on each platform. The posts go out on their own. You don't have to open the app or remember to post."

---

## Step 5 — Close with Business Outcome (2 minutes)

Say:

> "What this means practically: you spend 20 minutes on a Sunday evening filling in topics. The rest of the week, posts go out automatically. Your profile stays active, your audience sees you consistently, and you're not spending time on social media when you should be working with clients."

Ask:

> "Is consistent LinkedIn presence something that's currently taking time away from other work — or something you'd like to be doing more of but haven't been?"

---

## Pricing Conversation

> "I can set this up for you for ₹8,000–₹10,000. That covers the full system — content sheet, AI integration, scheduling connection, and a walkthrough so you know how to use it. After that, there's no ongoing cost unless you want monthly support."

---

## Objection Handling

**"Will the content sound like me?"**
> "The system uses your topics and your preferred tone. After the first batch, you'll give me feedback on what to adjust — within 2–3 rounds, the output reads naturally in your voice."

**"I don't post that regularly — will this be worth it?"**
> "That's exactly why this helps. The system removes the effort barrier. Once it's set up, you'll post more — because it takes 2 minutes instead of 30."

**"What platforms does it work on?"**
> "LinkedIn and Instagram to start. If you need Twitter/X or others, we can add those."
