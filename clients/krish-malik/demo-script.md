# Krish Malik — Demo Script

## Demo Name: White-Label AI Automation Partnership

## Duration: 20 minutes

## Goal: Show an agency owner how the white-label model adds value to their existing client relationships and creates a new high-margin revenue stream without technical investment.

---

## Setup Before the Demo

- One polished automation demo ready (lead flow or content scheduler, depending on their client base)
- A simple one-page partnership model overview
- Optional: a mock agency-branded output to show how the delivery looks

---

## Step 1 — Open with the Agency's Client Base (3 minutes)

Start with:

> "What types of businesses do most of your clients run — are they typically product brands, service businesses, or a mix?"

And:

> "After a branding or marketing project wraps up — do your clients come back with follow-on work, or does the relationship tend to end there?"

Let them describe their client lifecycle. Then:

> "The pattern I see with agencies is that clients need more services after the initial project — but they shop around for them rather than coming back to the same agency. Automation is one of those services. And the agencies that offer it are keeping clients longer and charging more per account."

---

## Step 2 — Explain the White-Label Model (5 minutes)

Walk through the structure:

> "Here's what I'm proposing. When one of your clients needs automation — lead handling, WhatsApp responses, content scheduling — you brief me. I build it in 5–7 days. It's delivered under your agency's name. You charge your client ₹20,000–₹30,000. You pay me the build cost of ₹8,000–₹12,000. The margin stays with you."

Draw or describe the flow:

```
Your client → Needs automation
You → Brief consultant
Consultant → Builds in 5–7 days
Delivered → Under your agency brand
You → Keep ₹10,000–₹18,000 per project
```

> "No technical hiring. No new tools for you to learn. You just add automation to what you already offer."

---

## Step 3 — Run a Relevant Demo (7 minutes)

Based on their clients, pick the most relevant demo:

**If they work with product/D2C brands:**
Show the WhatsApp lead follow-up system — a customer enquiry handled automatically, lead logged, follow-up triggered.

**If they work with content creators or personal brands:**
Show the content scheduling system — topic to caption to scheduled post in 3 minutes.

**If they work with B2B service companies:**
Show the Gmail lead parser — emails parsed, logged, and prioritised automatically.

Say:

> "This is the kind of system you'd deliver to one of your clients. The build takes one week. Your client gets something that directly saves them 1–2 hours a day. And you've added a meaningful service to your portfolio."

---

## Step 4 — Propose a Pilot (3 minutes)

> "Here's what I'd suggest as a starting point: no commitment, no formal arrangement. Pick one current client who has a WhatsApp or content problem. Let me build it. You deliver it under your agency's brand. We use that one project to see how the working model feels for both of us."

---

## Step 5 — Close (2 minutes)

> "I'm not looking to take on 10 agency partners. I'm looking for one or two whose client base is a good fit for this kind of automation. If the pilot project works well, we formalise the arrangement. If it doesn't, nothing lost."

Ask:

> "Is there a specific client you're thinking of right now who this would be relevant for?"

---

## Objection Handling

**"We're not a tech agency — our clients don't expect this from us."**
> "That's actually an advantage. When they get automation from their trusted branding agency, the relationship deepens. You're not being a tech company — you're being the partner that always has the next solution."

**"What if the client asks technical questions I can't answer?"**
> "I provide you with a simple Q&A brief for each project type. You don't need to know how it works — you just need to know what problem it solves and what outcome it delivers."

**"How do we handle support after delivery?"**
> "I provide a 2-week support period after every project. For ongoing support, there's a monthly retainer that can be included in the client package — another recurring revenue line for your agency."
